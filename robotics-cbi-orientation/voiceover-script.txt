Welcome to the Certificate in Robotics, Autonomous Systems and Intelligent Drone Technologies.

This fully online EQF Level 6 program is designed for learners who want to understand, design, simulate, and evaluate the next generation of robotic and autonomous systems.

Across two years, you will build a foundation in robotics, drones, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, swarm systems, environmental monitoring, infrastructure robotics, and future technologies.

The certificate carries 60 ECTS credits and runs over two academic years, with three terms per year.

The learning journey moves from foundations to advanced intelligent systems, then culminates in a capstone project where you design and evaluate an autonomous solution for a real-world challenge.

Because the program is fully online, practical learning is supported through simulation-based labs, data workflows, design exercises, and collaborative projects.

By the end of the certificate, students will be able to develop expertise in robotics, drones, and autonomous systems.

They will apply artificial intelligence and machine learning in robotic environments, understand swarm intelligence and coordinated autonomous systems, and analyze cybersecurity risks in drone and robotic ecosystems.

The program also introduces ethical and technological questions around drone warfare, quantum computing concepts relevant to robotics and AI, and sustainable design principles for environmental and infrastructure applications.

Year 1 builds the core foundation.

In Term 1, students study robotics, intelligent systems, drones, aerial robotics, and control design fundamentals.

In Term 2, the focus shifts to autonomy, sensor integration, swarm robotics, environmental monitoring, GIS, IoT, real-time data collection, and disaster-response drone coordination.

In Term 3, students connect artificial intelligence with robotics and drone intelligence, then examine cybersecurity and decision-making under uncertainty.

Year 2 moves into advanced intelligent systems and industry use.

Students explore infrastructure robotics, smart inspection systems, aerial additive manufacturing, robotic construction, autonomous repair, smart infrastructure, computer vision, AI-assisted drone perception, and future autonomous systems.

The curriculum also introduces quantum computing concepts, including quantum algorithms, optimization for swarm systems, quantum-enhanced AI, and quantum cybersecurity awareness.

The final term is the capstone project.

Students plan and execute a full robotics project lifecycle. They may design an environmental monitoring system, an infrastructure robotics solution, or another intelligent autonomous system that integrates perception, control, AI-driven decision-making, cybersecurity awareness, and sustainability.

The capstone asks students to evaluate performance in complex environments and present professional technical reports and demonstrations.

This certificate prepares learners to think across systems: mechanical design, control, perception, autonomy, data, risk, ethics, and sustainability.

The goal is not only to understand intelligent robotics, but to design autonomous technologies that are useful, secure, responsible, and ready for real-world challenges.
