Certificate in Robotics, Autonomous Systems and Intelligent Drone Technologies

A fully online EQF Level 6 certificate for learners designing responsible, secure, and sustainable autonomous systems.

AI
Welcome to the Certificate in Robotics, Autonomous Systems and Intelligent Drone Technologies.
This fully online EQF Level 6 program is designed for learners who want to understand, design, simulate, and evaluate next-generation robotic and autonomous systems.
Across two years, you will build a foundation in robotics, drones, AI, cybersecurity, swarm systems, environmental monitoring, infrastructure robotics, and future technologies.
Program Shape

Two years. Six terms. One applied capstone.

The structure moves from core foundations to advanced intelligent systems, then into a real-world autonomous solution.

The certificate carries 60 ECTS credits and runs over two academic years, with three terms per year.
The learning journey moves from foundations to advanced intelligent systems, then culminates in a capstone project for a real-world challenge.
Because the program is fully online, practical learning is supported through simulation-based labs, data workflows, design exercises, and collaborative projects.

Year 1 Foundation

From motion and control to autonomy, AI, and risk.

Term ICore Robotics, Drones and Control
  • Kinematics, dynamics, sensors and actuators
  • Drone components and flight control
  • Simulation and basic control strategies
Term IIAutonomy, Swarms and Environmental Monitoring
  • Sensor integration and real-time data
  • Swarm intelligence and coordination
  • GIS, IoT and disaster-response drones
Term IIIAI, Cybersecurity and Decision-Making
  • AI-driven drone navigation
  • Probabilistic models and Bayesian reasoning
  • GPS spoofing, jamming and swarm security
Year 1 builds the core foundation.
In Term 1, students study robotics, intelligent systems, drones, aerial robotics, and control design fundamentals.
Term 2 shifts to autonomy, sensor integration, swarm robotics, environmental monitoring, GIS, IoT, real-time data collection, and disaster-response drone coordination.
In Term 3, students connect AI with robotics and drone intelligence, then examine cybersecurity and decision-making under uncertainty.
Year 2 Advanced Systems

Advanced intelligent systems, industry use, and future technologies.

Term IIPerception, AI and Quantum Concepts
  • Computer vision and object detection

Year 2 moves into advanced intelligent systems and industry use.
Students explore infrastructure robotics, smart inspection, aerial additive manufacturing, robotic construction, autonomous repair, smart infrastructure, computer vision, and drone perception.
The curriculum also introduces quantum computing concepts, including quantum algorithms, swarm optimization, quantum-enhanced AI, and quantum cybersecurity awareness.

The capstone integrates perception, control, AI-driven decision-making, cybersecurity awareness, sustainability, evaluation, and professional technical reporting.
