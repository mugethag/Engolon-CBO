Big idea

Module 1 · Introduction to Sustainability Robotics

A robot is a system that senses, decides, acts, and uses feedback to improve its behavior. Sustainability robotics applies these capabilities to environmental, infrastructure, and community challenges.

The robotics loop

1
Sense Collect information from the environment using sensors.
2
Decide Process that information and choose an action.
3
Act Move or interact with the environment using actuators.
4
Feedback Compare actual behavior with the desired outcome.
5
Correct Adjust future commands based on feedback.
Step 5 feeds back into Step 1 — the loop repeats continuously.

Core terms

Sensor

A device that collects information from the robot or its environment. Examples: camera, ultrasonic distance sensor, temperature probe.

Actuator

A component that creates motion or physical action. Examples: electric motor, servo, hydraulic piston, propeller.

Controller

The decision-making component that receives sensor data and sends commands to the actuators.

Feedback

Information about actual performance used to adjust future action. Without feedback, errors accumulate uncorrected.

Kinematics

The study of motion, position, velocity, and path — without considering the forces that cause them.

Dynamics

The study of forces, torque, mass, acceleration, and energy — the physics behind why a robot moves the way it does.

Open-loop control

Control that sends a command without checking the result. Simple but can't correct errors. Example: a timer-based sprinkler.

Closed-loop control

Control that uses feedback to compare actual vs. desired behavior and makes corrections. Example: a thermostat.

Sustainability robotics

Robotics designed to address environmental, social, and resource-aware challenges — minimising harm and maximising community benefit.

Bio-inspired robotics

Robotic design influenced by living systems. Examples: drones modelled on bird flight, swarm robots modelled on ant colonies.
