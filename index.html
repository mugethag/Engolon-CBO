Our Name Means

Engolon strength, power, energy

A Maasai word expressing physical strength, capability, and intensity. Used in cultural mottos to denote resilience and power—as in "Naboisho ang, engolon ang" meaning "Our unity, our strength"

Engolon Community Based Organization is a Kenya-centered organization with a global audience, dedicated to empowering vulnerable populations through skills training, financial support, education, and access to essential services. Every person deserves the opportunity to thrive.

Youth Sports & Mentorship

Building confidence, discipline, and community through skateboarding and inline skating on Kibera's streets

Our youth sports program uses skateboarding and inline skating as a vehicle for mentorship and character development. Older youth mentor younger children — learning leadership while passing on skills. Every session on Kibera's brick streets is a lesson in perseverance, trust, and belonging.

Experience Kibera: Community Tours

Designed by residents, for respectful visitors. Every tour directly funds Engolon's community programs.

🤝 Community-led 💚 Proceeds fund local programs 🙏 Dignity-first guidelines

These tours are designed by and for Kibera's community. Visitors are welcomed as guests, not spectators. Our local guides set the tone — respectful, honest, and human. Photography and behaviour guidelines are shared before every tour.

🏘️

Cultural & Community Experience

Meet local residents, learn about daily life, and visit small businesses and community projects. Authentic insights from those who live here.

  • 📍 3–4 hours, community-guided
  • 👥 Groups of 4–10 people
  • 💰 $25 USD per person
🎨

Arts, Crafts & Creativity Tour

Discover local artists and artisans. Watch traditional crafts being made, support emerging creatives, and take home authentic handmade pieces.

  • 📍 2.5–3 hours, artist-guided
  • 👥 Groups of 3–8 people
  • 💰 $30 USD per person
🍲

Food Tour & Community Stories

Share meals and stories with residents at family-run eateries. Learn about food culture, livelihoods, and the people behind the food.

  • 📍 3–4 hours, resident-hosted
  • 👥 Groups of 4–12 people
  • 💰 $35 USD per person (includes meals)
📚

Youth & Education Tour

Visit youth programs in action. Meet young mentors and learners, see the challenges and the breakthroughs, and understand what community-led education looks like.

  • 📍 2–3 hours, youth-led
  • 👥 Groups of 5–15 people
  • 💰 $20 USD per person
